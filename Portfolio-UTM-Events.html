Data Governance & Global Taxonomy


Eliminating "Data Debt" through Scalable Tracking Frameworks


1. The Problem: The High Cost of Messy Data

In many organizations, marketing data becomes "junk" because of inconsistent tagging, lack of naming conventions, and improper event tracking. This leads to:

  • Attribution Black Holes: GA4 being unable to assign traffic (showing as "Unassigned").
  • Inflated Traffic: Internal clicks triggering new sessions.
  • Reporting Fatigue: Teams spending hours "cleaning" data instead of analyzing it.

I engineered a unified Data Governance Framework to standardize how interactions are captured and categorized across web, app, and offline channels.


2. The UTM Strategy: Precision Attribution

I developed a strict UTM (Urchin Traffic Monitor) standard to ensure 100% attribution accuracy across Email, Paid Social, Affiliate, and Offline campaigns.


Core Governance Rules:

  • Source vs. Medium Integrity: Established a "Who vs. How" logic (e.g., Source: linkedin, Medium: paid_social) to prevent the common error of mixing these fields.
  • Syntax Standardization: Enforced a snake_case only policy (all lowercase, underscores for spaces) to eliminate "case-sensitive duplication."
  • Platform-Specific Forensics: Documented specific fail-points for Meta’s dynamic tags and established manual overrides to ensure campaign IDs always populate correctly.

3. The Event Taxonomy: Scalable GTM Architecture

To move beyond basic "clicks," I designed a hierarchical Event and Tag Taxonomy based on an Object + Action framework. This ensures that any team member can look at a report and immediately understand the user interaction.


Taxonomy Architecture:

  • Hierarchy Design: Events follow a logical descent (e.g., click → click_nav → click_nav_cta). This makes reports intuitive and allows for high-level roll-up analysis.
  • GTM Master Tagging Strategy: Instead of building hundreds of individual tags, I architected "Master Tags" in Google Tag Manager. By using dynamic variables, a single tag can listen for and categorize multiple interaction types.
  • Variable Governance: Established strict naming for GTM Variables (e.g., GA4-measurement_id) to ensure the container remains organized.

4. Business Impact & Scalability

This framework was designed to be Industry-Agnostic, with specific versions tailored for B2C, B2B, and E-commerce models.

  • Onboarding Efficiency: New team members can understand the entire data ecosystem in minutes rather than weeks.
  • Reliable Attribution: By standardizing the "Source/Medium" logic, we reduced "Unassigned" traffic in GA4 by nearly 20%.
  • Future-Proofing: The taxonomy is built over GA4’s default events, ensuring that future platform updates don't break the existing data pipeline.
