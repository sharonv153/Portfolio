RFM Analytics & Lifecycle Research


Recovering $40,500 in "At Risk" Revenue through an 8-Day Strategic SQL Sprint


1. The Business Challenge

A luxury e-commerce brand sought to increase revenue without increasing its dependence on paid advertising. Historical data showed that while the brand had a database of over 11,000 leads and customers, only 58.65% had ever made a purchase. Of those who did buy, 82% were one-time shoppers.


I was tasked with identifying "revenue leakage" and building a technical framework to reactivate high-value customers. The project was executed from initial exploratory research to final strategic recommendations in a comprehensive 8-day sprint.


2. Data Methodology: The 2.5-Year Historical Audit

Most standard e-commerce audits utilize a 12-month window. For this project, I engineered a 2.5-year lookback period to capture the true story of customer decay in a high-ticket, low-frequency vertical:

  • Establishing "Data Density": With an 88% churn rate, a shorter window would have resulted in statistically insignificant sample sizes. Expanding to 2.5 years revealed a $40,509.47 recovery opportunity in the "At Risk" segment that a standard audit would have missed.
  • Defining the "Churn Threshold":By analyzing the "Lost" segment, I calculated a 27-day "Golden Window" for retention. If a second purchase doesn't occur within this window, the probability of return drops exponentially.

3. Technical Execution & Multi-Platform ETL Pipeline

The core of this project was an end-to-end data pipeline built under significant administrative constraints. The 8-day timeline allowed for a rigorous transition from raw research to structured cloud architecture.


Phase 1: Exploratory Research & Forensic Data Cleaning (Days 1-3)

  • Discovery: Analyzed the Shopify database to identify that 4,800+ leads had never purchased, representing a massive untapped conversion pool.
  • Data Sanitization:Handled significant formatting issues within raw CSV exports after a 10-day permission delay for direct connectors.
    • Completely removed disruptive commas from product names/descriptions to prevent BigQuery schema breakage.
    • De-nested complex product tags into relational columns to enable future cohort analysis.
    • Scrubbed test transactions and "EXC-" (exchange) orders to ensure a pure revenue dataset.

Phase 2: Infrastructure & SQL Architecture (Days 4-7)

  • Cloud Integration: Stored sanitized datasets in a GCP Cloud Storage Bucket and linked BigQuery datasets via gsutil URIs.
  • Logic Engineering: Spent 4 days developing and troubleshooting the SQL scoring models. Because 83% of the customer base had a frequency of 1, standard quintile functions were insufficient.
  • Hybrid Scoring: Engineered a custom CASE logic to manually weight repeat purchasers (1, 2, 3, 4, 5+), while using NTILE(5) for Recency and Monetary quintiles.

RFM Segmentation Query

BigQuery view creation for customer RFM (Recency, Frequency, Monetary) scoring

/*Step 3: with the query working now we can create a VIEW that we can use to classify users based on their score with a new query*/
              CREATE VIEW `cherie-amour-lingerie-shop.shopify_raw.RFM_scores`
              AS (
              /* Step 2:Calculate the RFM scores from the original query by nesting it*/
                SELECT email,
                  recency,
                  frequency,
                  monetary,
                  NTILE(5) OVER (ORDER BY recency ASC) AS R_score,
                  /* Manual F_score logic for our highly skewed data where 83% clients frequency = 1*/
                  CASE
                    WHEN frequency >= 5 THEN 5
                    WHEN frequency = 4 THEN 4
                    WHEN frequency = 3 THEN 3
                    WHEN frequency = 2 THEN 2
                    WHEN frequency = 1 THEN 1
                  END AS F_score,
                  NTILE(5) OVER (ORDER BY monetary DESC) AS M_score
                FROM (
                  /*Step 1: Calulate the recency, frequency, and monetary values*/
                  SELECT email, DATE_DIFF(CURRENT_TIMESTAMP(),
                  max(created_at), DAY) AS recency ,
                  count(distinct(Order_Number)) AS frequency,
                  sum(Subtotal) AS monetary,
                  FROM `cherie-amour-lingerie-shop.shopify_raw.orders`
                  WHERE NOT REGEXP_CONTAINS(Order_Number, R'^EXC-')
                  GROUP BY email)
              );

Phase 3: Strategic Translation & Final Reporting (Day 8)

  • Financial Modeling: Aggregated scores into a custom segmentation view to quantify "Lost Revenue" per tier.
  • Recommendation Mapping: Translated raw clusters into actionable marketing plays for Champions, Loyalists, and At-Risk segments.

Customer Segmentation Logic

BigQuery view that classifies customers into segments based on RFM scores

/* Segmentation query*/
            CREATE VIEW `cherie-amour-lingerie-shop.shopify_raw.RFM_segments`
            AS (
            SELECT email,
              CONCAT (R_score,'-', F_score,'-',M_score) AS rfm_points,
              CASE
                -- 1. Champions
                WHEN R_score = 5 AND F_score = 5 AND M_score = 5 THEN 'Champions'
                -- 2. Loyalists: High-value repeaters.
                WHEN R_score >= 3 AND F_score >= 3 AND M_score >= 3 THEN 'Loyalists'
                -- 3. At Risk: High-value past customers who stopped buying.
                WHEN R_score <= 2 AND F_score >= 3 THEN 'At Risk'
                -- 4. Potential Loyalists: Recent buyers with high spend (M>=3) but low frequency.
                WHEN R_score >= 4 AND M_score >= 3 AND F_score <= 2 THEN 'Potential Loyalist'
                -- 5. Promising / Recent: Recent buyers, but lower spend/frequency.
                WHEN R_score >= 4 AND F_score <= 2 THEN 'Promising'
                -- 6. About to Sleep: Middle-of-the-road customers drifting away.
                WHEN R_score = 3 THEN 'About to Sleep'
                -- 7. Hibernating: Low recency (2), low everything else.
                WHEN R_score = 2 THEN 'Hibernating'
                -- 8. Lost: The bottom of the barrel.
                WHEN R_score = 1 AND F_score = 1 AND M_score = 1 THEN 'Lost'
                -- 9. Can't Lose Them: Low recency but high historical frequency.
                WHEN R_score = 1 AND F_score >= 4 THEN 'Cannot Lose Them'
                -- If they are R=1 and not high frequency, they are truly lost.    
                ELSE 'Lost'
              END AS customer_segment
            FROM `cherie-amour-lingerie-shop.shopify_raw.RFM_scores`
            ORDER BY customer_segment
            );

Key Discovery: The Gateway Price Point

Detailed analysis of the Champions vs. Lost segments revealed a critical pricing correlation:

  • Champions almost always started their journey with "Gateway" products priced below $50.
  • High-Ticket Churn: Customers who started with items priced above $65 had the highest churn rates, indicating that lower-priced entry points are the primary drivers of long-term LTV for this brand.

5. Strategic Recommendations & Future Research

  1. Acquisition Pivot: Shift top-of-funnel ads to focus on "Gateway" products (< $50) to maximize the probability of a second purchase.
  2. The 30-Day Retention Sprint: Implement high-urgency nurturing flows within the 30-day "Golden Window" identified in the churn analysis.
  3. Segment-Specific Nudging: Data Layer Architecture, Master Tag Logic.
    • At Risk: Focus on "Gift with Purchase" (GWP) strategies to appeal to this segment's preference for high unit counts.
    • Hibernating: Re-introduce the brand with "New Arrival" collections to bridge the 24-month gap.
  4. Future Research:Analyze the correlation between shipping costs and AOV to identify the maximum threshold for "Free Shipping" that increases repeat order probability.
