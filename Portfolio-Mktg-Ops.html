Challenges of Data Tracking in a Niche Industry


Company Overview

Cherie Amour (CA) is a nationwide boudoir photography company with a unique business model. Unlike traditional studios with fixed locations, CA operates with a team of traveling photographers who serve clients across 55+ cities. To maintain a premium experience, the company rents suites in luxury hotels where the photoshoots take place.


Technology Stack

To support operations and marketing, CA leveraged a combination of industry-specific and general digital tools:

Additional Marketing Tools:

  • CRM & Booking System: MyStratus by Studio Plus – CRM tailored for photography businesses
  • Marketing & Automation: Klaviyo for email marketing and lead nurturing
  • Website Management: WordPress as the content management system
  • Analytics & Tracking: GA4 & Google Tag Manager (GTM)
  • SEO Optimization: Google Search Console & SEMrush
  • Advertising Platforms: Meta Ads & Google Ads

The Challenge

Data Tracking & Conversion Attribution

A major limitation of MyStratus was its lack of integration with common analytics and advertising tools like GA4, Meta Pixel, or Klaviyo. Without an open API, accurately tracking online conversions proved difficult, impacting the ability to optimize ad spend and improve marketing efficiency.

Without reliable tracking, key challenges included:

  • Inability to track conversions and abandoned carts
  • Inefficient ad spend due to continued targeting of converted users
  • Limited visibility into lead engagement and sales attribution

After consulting with the software vendor, it became evident that a direct GA4 integration was not feasible. As an alternative, I developed a workaround using GTM, Klaviyo, unique coupon codes, and CRM exports to track user behavior and optimize campaign performance.


The Solution

Alternative Conversion Tracking Methods


Step 1: Implementing GTM for Conversion Tracking

Since MyStratus used an external booking platform, traditional tracking methods were not an option. To capture conversions, I implemented a redirect to a "Thank You" page on our website immediately after booking.

GTM Configuration:

Trigger: "Stratus to Thank You"

  • Fires on Page URL containing /thank-you-for-booking
  • Referrer contains cherieamour.mystratus.com

Tag: "Booking Completed"

  • Measurement ID: {{GA4 id}}
  • Event name: booking_complete
  • Event parameters:
    • event_category: booking
    • event_label: online_booking

This approach successfully recorded completed bookings as conversions in GA4. However, it had limitations—events occurring within the MyStratus platform (such as cart abandonments) remained untracked, requiring additional data sources for funnel visibility.


The chart above shows events in the purple boxed that happen within CA's website that can be tracked. Events in the yellow box show events within myStratus' website that cannot be tracked.

Step 2: Creating Segmented Lead Lists in Klaviyo

To track conversion paths effectively, I established three Klaviyo lead lists:

  • List 1:Leads from Meta ad landing pages
  • List 2: Leads from Google ad landing pages
  • List 3: Recent online bookings imported from MyStratus

This segmentation allowed for precise analysis of lead sources and engagement levels.

Step 3: Capturing Key Data Fields

To improve attribution tracking, I created three custom fields in Klaviyo:

  • Status: Identifies whether a user remains a lead or has converted
  • Client Id: Unique identifier assigned upon booking
  • City: Preferred photoshoot location

Additionally, hidden fields in signup forms captured:

  • UTM Campaign: Associates leads with specific campaigns
  • Source: Identifies whether they came from Google or Meta Ads
  • Platform: Differentiates between Instant Forms, Lead Forms, and landing page signups

Step 4: Syncing Lead Lists with Advertising Platforms

Klaviyo’s integrations enabled seamless syncing with Meta and Google Ads. Once a user subscribed, their entry triggered:

  • Removal from middle-of-funnel (MOFU) campaigns
  • Inclusion in bottom-of-funnel (BOFU) campaigns for higher-intent targeting

With lists and segments connected, I automated audience exclusions, ensuring that converted users no longer received conversion-focused ads.


Tracking Lead Conversions & Audience Segmentation

To dynamically update audience targeting, I established two segments for each lead list:

  • Converted Clients: Users whose Status field updated to "Client"
  • Unconverted Leads: Users without a Status field update

With these segments, I could:

  1. Exclude converted users from ad campaigns
  2. Enroll converted users into a nurturing email sequence
  3. Build lookalike audiences for future prospecting


Data Flow from Lead to Client Status.

Using the CAPI Method for Meta Ads

To enhance accuracy, I implemented Meta’s Conversions API (CAPI) for direct data transfer from Klaviyo to Meta, enabling:

  • More precise event tracking
  • Reduced reliance on third-party cookies
  • Automated exclusion of converted users

For omnichannel campaigns, I maintain separate lists for different platforms (Meta, Google, Pinterest) and update the Source field using profile property flows. This ensures that even when users move through segments, we can still track their original entry point.


Results & Key Takeaways

Pros:

  1. Enabled structured tracking despite CRM limitations
  2. Improved segmentation and ad targeting
  3. Supported omnichannel marketing by integrating multiple data sources
  4. Leveraged Klaviyo’s automation capabilities

Cons:

  1. Required manual data uploads, increasing workload
  2. Introduced a lag in conversion tracking, affecting real-time optimizations
  3. Could not track abandoned carts within MyStratus

Final Process Implementation

To maintain accurate data flow, I regularly:

  1. Exported recent online bookings from MyStratus
  2. Updated the Status field for booked users in Klaviyo
  3. Ensured sync between Klaviyo, Meta, and Google Ads for accurate audience targeting

Although not as seamless as a native integration, this approach significantly improved conversion tracking, audience segmentation, and budget efficiency. By strategically combining GTM, Klaviyo, and CAPI, I developed a data-driven marketing framework that enhanced campaign effectiveness within the constraints of a niche CRM.
