Challenges of Data Tracking in a Niche Industry

Company Overview

Cherie Amour (CA) is a nationwide boudoir photography company with a unique business model. Unlike traditional studios with fixed locations, CA operates with a team of traveling photographers who serve clients across 55+ cities. To maintain a premium experience, the company rents suites in luxury hotels where the photoshoots take place.


Technology Stack

As a photography company, Cherie Amour relies on Studio Plus and its CRM software, myStratus, which is tailored for photographers and studios. This CRM enables CA to track client data, automate marketing efforts, monitor sales, generate reports, and manage online bookings. Additionally, Studio Plus offers InspiredByYou, an online platform where clients can book appointments, make payments, and view and purchase their photos.

Additional Marketing Tools:

  • Klaviyo – Email marketing and automation
  • WordPress – CMS for the company website
  • GA4 & GTM – Analytics and tracking
  • Google Search Console & SEMrush – SEO optimization
  • Meta Ads & Google Ads – Advertising platforms

The Challenge

Data Tracking & Conversion Attribution

One major limitation of myStratus is its lack of integration with common analytics and advertising tools like GA4, Meta Pixel, or Klaviyo. Without an open API, it’s difficult to track online conversions accurately, making it challenging to optimize ad spending and improve marketing efficiency.

For a marketer, this presents a significant problem. If I can’t track conversions, abandoned carts, or lead engagement, our advertising campaigns risk inefficiencies—continuing to serve ads to users who have already converted and missing out on high-intent prospects.

After consulting with the software vendor, we explored a GTM tag implementation for GA4. However, despite multiple troubleshooting attempts, this method was unsuccessful. With no direct way to track conversions, we needed to develop a creative workaround.


The Solution

Alternative Conversion Tracking Methods

To bridge the gap, I implemented a manual tracking solution using Klaviyo, unique coupon codes, and CRM exports. While not as immediate or precise as native tracking integrations, this approach allowed me to:

  • Identify users at different funnel stages
  • Improve retargeting efforts
  • Optimize ad spend by excluding converted users

Step 1: Creating Segmented Lead Lists in Klaviyo

To track conversion paths effectively, I created three new Klaviyo lead lists, each tied to a specific source:

  • List 1: Leads from Google Ads
  • List 2: Leads from Meta Ads (via Instant Forms)
  • List 3: Leads from our landing pages linked to ads

Since each campaign uses separate landing pages tailored to specific audiences, this structure provided granular insights into where users engaged most.

Step 2: Capturing Key Data Fields

To ensure proper tracking, I added three custom fields to each Klaviyo list:

  • Status: Tracks whether a user is still a lead or has converted into a client
  • Client: A unique identifier assigned once they book a session
  • City: Their preferred photoshoot location

Additionally, I used hidden fields within signup forms to collect campaign attribution data:

  • UTM Campaign: Associates leads with specific campaigns
  • Source: Identifies whether they came from Google or Meta Ads
  • Platform: Differentiates between Meta Instant Forms, Google Lead Forms, or landing page signups

Step 3: Syncing Lead Lists with Advertising Platforms

Klaviyo’s integrations allowed me to connect lead lists to advertising platforms. Once a user subscribed to a list, their entry was logged as a conversion event, removing them from middle-of-funnel (MOF) campaigns and pushing them into bottom-of-funnel (BOF) campaigns for higher-intent targeting.


Tracking Lead Conversions & Audience Segmentation

Once a lead enters our system, they are enrolled in an email workflow designed to nurture them with promotions, educational content, and next steps.

To refine our audience targeting, I built two Klaviyo segments:

  1. Converted Clients: Users whose status updates to "Client"
  2. Unconverted Leads: Users in campaign lists but with an empty status field

How This Works in Practice:

  • Leads with an empty Status field remain in BOF retargeting campaigns.
  • Once their Status updates to "Client," they are removed from conversion-focused ads and added to the Social Converted segment for continued engagement.

For omnichannel campaigns, I maintain separate lists for different platforms (Meta, Google, Pinterest) and update the Source field using profile property flows. This ensures that even when users move through segments, we can still track their original entry point.

Finalizing the Process & Cross-Referencing Data

With all lists, segments, and automations in place, we can now launch ad campaigns that dynamically adjust based on user engagement. To keep the audience targeting updated:

  1. Regularly export and import bookings from myStratus into the "Client Converted" list in Klaviyo.
  2. Update the Status field for booked users to "Client."
  3. Ensure Klaviyo, Meta, and Google Ads remain synced for audience exclusions.

Using the CAPI Method for Meta Ads

By leveraging Meta’s Conversions API (CAPI), I automate conversion tracking. This method enables:

  • Direct data transfer from Klaviyo to Meta
  • Better event tracking accuracy
  • Reduced reliance on third-party cookies

To implement this, I configured Klaviyo to pass conversion data from the "Client Converted" list to Meta, signaling when a lead has become a customer. This prevents ads from being served to already-converted users, optimizing budget allocation.


Pros & Cons of This Workaround

Pros:

  • Provides a structured way to track leads and conversions without native integrations
  • Enables better segmentation and more precise ad targeting
  • Works well for omnichannel campaigns by integrating multiple data sources
  • Leverages Klaviyo’s extensive integrations for further automation

Cons:

  • Requires manual data uploads, making it time-consuming
  • Has a lag in conversion tracking, impacting real-time campaign adjustments
  • Does not track cart abandonments, limiting some remarketing efforts

Key Takeaways

Although this workaround is not as seamless as a direct CRM-to-ad platform integration, it significantly improves conversion tracking, audience segmentation, and budget efficiency. By combining Klaviyo, custom lists, and CAPI, we ensure that our marketing campaigns remain data-driven and effective, even within the constraints of a niche CRM.
