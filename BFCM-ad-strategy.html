BFCM 2025 Ad Strategy
High-Efficiency Acquisition

A 3-phase Meta advertising campaign built to capture leads ahead of Black Friday, nurture intent during the pre-sale window, and convert during peak week — achieving a CPL of $2.35 against an industry benchmark of $4–$20.

Meta Ads Klaviyo Lead Scoring Oct – Dec 2025 $1,977 Total Spend
01
Executive Summary

This campaign was designed and executed for a boudoir photography studio operating across multiple U.S. cities during the 2025 Black Friday / Cyber Monday season. The strategy addressed a core challenge specific to high-ticket service businesses: the full revenue value of a campaign is not visible at the time of the sale — it unfolds over a 30–45 day fulfillment window as booked sessions are completed and product sales are processed.

Rather than running a single promotional push, the strategy was structured as a 3-phase funnel — building awareness and audience data in October, remarketing to warm audiences with offer teasers in early November, and converting during BFCM week with urgency-driven availability ads. The campaign also complemented a separate Klaviyo email strategy, with ad audiences and email subscribers managed in coordination throughout each phase.

Campaign Output

825 leads collected at $2.35 CPL  ·  16 bookings (11 effective after cancellations)  ·  $7,370 revenue earned  ·  $10,384 projected revenue  ·  0.81 projected ROI on $1,977 total spend as of February 16, 2026.

Why immediate ROI understates campaign value: This business model involves a 30–45 day gap between booking and final sale appointment. Session fees are collected upfront, but the high-ticket product sale — averaging ~$1,200 AOV — occurs after the client views their gallery. Any ROI figure taken at or shortly after BFCM week captures only a fraction of the revenue the campaign has already secured in the pipeline.

03
3-Phase Campaign Strategy

The strategy was built on a sequenced funnel model: warm audiences before you sell to them. Each phase had a distinct objective, budget, targeting logic, and output that fed directly into the next phase.

Phase 01 Awareness
3–4 weeks pre-BFCM · October

Reach maximum people with cheap awareness ads. Show full pricing — no discounts — to anchor value before any offer is revealed. Simultaneously collect audience data through landing page visits, video engagement, and lead form interactions for Phase 2 retargeting.

Ad Groups
  • Experience packages with full pricing
  • BTS video + experience showcase
  • Package carousel (retargeted to video viewers)
  • Social proof + "why us" graphics
  • Limited spots CTA for specific cities
$30/day ≈ $420/mo · Meta only · Awareness objective
Phase 02 Remarketing
1–2 weeks pre-BFCM · Early November

Remarket exclusively to Phase 1 engagers. Tease 1–2 BFCM offers without revealing full details. Drive email sign-ups with an early-access hook. Split ad groups by geography (East / West) to surface city-specific availability.

Ad Groups
  • Noir package tease + Meta native lead form
  • Noir tease + redirect to landing page
  • East cities: experience + blurred early offer
  • West cities: experience + blurred early offer
  • Social proof + perks carousel
$21/day ≈ $294/mo · Awareness & click objective
Phase 03 Conversion
BFCM Week · Late November

Full conversion push to Phase 1 & 2 audiences. Mon–Thu: email subscribers receive exclusive early access while ads surface availability countdowns by city. Fri onward: urgency copy with "X spots remaining." Coordinated with Klaviyo email sequences throughout.

Ad Groups
  • Holiday Offer + Noir Package promotion
  • Free product over $750 orders
  • East spots availability carousel
  • West spots availability carousel
  • Experience video + reviews + "what you get"
$50/day ≈ $700/mo · Remarketing & click objective
Strategic rationale

Spending cheaply in Phase 1 to build warm audiences meant Phase 3 conversion spend was directed only at users who had already demonstrated interest — improving ad relevance, reducing CPL, and ensuring the selling phase worked with existing momentum. Email subscribers experienced privileged early access, increasing their conversion likelihood before ads went wide to all Phase 1 & 2 audiences.

04
Marketing Operations & Governance

Two operational systems were built alongside the ad strategy: a behavioral lead scoring layer in Klaviyo to prioritize follow-up, and a targeting governance policy to correct a platform-level budget misallocation that was silently degrading performance.

Lead Scoring in Klaviyo

A custom qualification system was implemented to surface high-intent leads for manual sales follow-up rather than treating all 825 leads as equal. The model combined two behavioral signals:

SignalBehaviorIntent Interpretation
Site Behavior Viewed /book-now page Active consideration — evaluating booking logistics and pricing
Email Engagement Opened + clicked nurture emails Sustained interest — not a passive or accidental sign-up

Leads who triggered both signals were flagged for direct personal outreach. Lower-scoring leads were managed through automated sequences. This prevented follow-up effort from being spread across all 825 contacts and ensured the highest-conversion prospects received the most personal attention during the booking window.

Meta Advantage+ Governance Policy

Finding: Meta's Advantage+ AI was identified as misallocating budget — serving ads to demographics with low purchase intent for high-ticket boudoir photography. The platform optimized for engagement signals that did not correlate with qualified lead behavior, creating a silent CPL drag that would worsen as spend increased.

A governance policy was established to override Advantage+ defaults and enforce stricter demographic control using Meta's original audience options. The policy was documented to ensure future campaigns begin with the correct configuration rather than diagnosing the same issue after budget has been wasted.

ParameterAdvantage+ DefaultGovernance Override
Age targetingBroad AI expansion allowedLocked to qualified age range
Gender targetingExpanded to all gendersRestricted to primary buyer demographic
Audience expansionAI-driven broadeningOriginal audiences only — no expansion
PlacementsAutomatic all placementsReviewed and pruned by performance data
Governance principle

Meta Advantage+ is optimized for scale with broad consumer audiences. For a niche, high-ticket service with a specific buyer profile, the platform's expansion logic actively works against CPL efficiency. Establishing this as a documented policy — not just a one-time fix — creates a replicable standard for all future campaigns and protects against default platform behavior reintroducing the same waste.

05
Key Learnings & Optimizations
Phase sequencing beats single-push campaigns Validated

Building warm audiences in Phase 1 before spending on conversion in Phase 3 was the primary driver of the $2.35 CPL. Cold-audience conversion campaigns during competitive BFCM conditions routinely produce CPLs 5–10× higher. The sequencing pre-qualified the audience before a dollar of conversion spend was committed.

ROI framing must match the business model Operational

For service businesses with a 30–45 day fulfillment delay, immediate post-campaign ROI is structurally misleading. A pipeline-aware model — separating earned revenue, pending appointments, and projected AOV — is the correct framework for evaluating performance and informing future budget decisions.

Platform AI requires active governance Policy

Meta Advantage+ optimizes for scale, not qualification. For niche, high-ticket services, leaving demographic targeting to the platform's AI introduces systematic budget waste. Governance policies — documented and enforced from campaign setup — are a requirement, not a preference.

Lead scoring enables follow-up precision Ops

825 leads is only valuable if follow-up is prioritized correctly. Behavioral scoring separated high-intent contacts from passive sign-ups, allowing personal sales outreach to be directed at the contacts with the highest conversion probability during the limited booking window.
